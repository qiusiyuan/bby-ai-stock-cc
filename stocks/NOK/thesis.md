# NOK — Nokia Oyj

## One-line thesis
Nokia is a network infrastructure play on 5G and AI data center buildout, not an AI company — it belongs on this list because optical networking and photonic chips are genuine infrastructure for AI, and at a ~$75B market cap the valuation is undemanding if Nokia can stabilize margins and win incremental AI-related contracts — *this thesis is wrong if* Nokia fails to sustain competitive wins against Ericsson and Huawei in 5G, the optical networking division stalls in AI data center deployments, or management continues to disappoint on the earnings recovery timeline.

## Snapshot
Communication Equipment · NYSE ADR (Finnish HQ, Espoo) · ~$75B cap · $13.49 (2026-06-18) · 52w range $4.00–$17.45 · up dramatically off lows

## What it does
Nokia is a B2B technology company selling network infrastructure, not a consumer brand. Its four segments are: **Mobile Networks** (radio access network equipment for telco operators; 5G base stations, antennas, software); **Network Infrastructure** (fiber/IP/optical transport equipment for telcos and increasingly data centers); **Cloud and Network Services** (software-defined networking, 5G core, private wireless); and **Nokia Technologies** (patent licensing — smartphones, IoT, consumer electronics). The Nokia phone brand is licensed out; Nokia itself does not sell consumer devices.

## Industry & TAM
Nokia operates across two growth vectors with different timing:

**5G RAN (Mobile Networks)** — the global 5G infrastructure market was worth ~$40B/yr at peak and is now in a post-initial-deployment slowdown. Nokia competes directly with Ericsson (Sweden), Huawei (China), and Samsung. Huawei's partial exclusion from Western markets has been a tailwind for Nokia; any reversal of that trend is a direct headwind.

**AI data center networking (Network Infrastructure, optical)** — this is the angle that justifies the `[ai]` tag. Data center interconnect (DCI) and optical transport for hyperscaler AI clusters is genuinely growing fast. Nokia's June 2026 announcement of a $30M expansion of its Pennsylvania photonic chip facility, explicitly tied to "AI-native networks," signals that management believes optical networking is a real AI infrastructure play. Nokia's IP routing products (7750 SR series) are widely deployed in hyperscaler fabrics.

## Why I'm watching — honest assessment
Nokia is a controversial inclusion on an AI list. This is not an AI company in the way Nvidia, AMD, or AVGO are. The honest case for Nokia in 2026 is:

1. **Valuation reset**: The stock ran from $4 to $17 on the 52w range — it has already been "discovered." At $13.49, the question is whether the earnings recovery is real or a head fake.
2. **Optical networking as AI infrastructure**: Nokia's photonic chip investment and data center interconnect products are genuine picks-and-shovels for AI clusters. This is not marketing spin — optical transport is a real bottleneck as hyperscalers scale to 100K+ GPU clusters.
3. **Defense exposure**: Nokia Defense (Banshee 5G deployable networks for military) is a genuine growth segment as European defense spending accelerates post-2022. The KNDS deal (June 2026) is an example.

The **bear case** for Nokia is that it has disappointed investors systematically for a decade. Huawei competition, 5G capex cycles, restructuring costs, and execution misses have defined the Nokia investing experience. New CEO Pekka Lundmark has been rebuilding since 2020 but results have been inconsistent.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (25%)**: Optical networking for AI data centers becomes a $5B+ Nokia segment by 2028; 5G market stabilizes; patent licensing holds firm; operating margin expands to 12–14%. Target $20+.
- **Base (50%)**: Nokia delivers 4–6% revenue CAGR; optical data center wins accumulate gradually; operating margin recovers to 10–12% from current depressed level. Target $16.
- **Bear (25%)**: 5G capex cycle deteriorates further in North America and India; optical wins are modest; Ericsson continues to outexecute; margin recovery stalls below 9%. Target $8–9.

## Moat
Nokia's moat is **narrow and contested**. There are real switching costs in RAN deployments — once a carrier has Nokia's radio equipment and OSS/BSS integrated, swapping vendors costs years and hundreds of millions — but this moat is shared equally with Ericsson, and Huawei's price-competitive products remain attractive in non-Western markets. The IP portfolio (Nokia Technologies) is a durable moat — Nokia holds strong 4G/5G SEP positions and collects recurring license income. Optical networking is less differentiated: Nokia, Ciena, Infinera, and Huawei all compete credibly. There is no compelling network effect, no cornered resource, and limited process power advantage over Ericsson in RAN.

## Management & capital allocation
Pekka Lundmark (CEO since 2020) has been restructuring the business — cutting headcount, exiting unprofitable contracts, and refocusing on higher-margin segments. Capital allocation has been conservative: a dividend is paid (unusual for a turnaround), share buybacks are modest. R&D spending is ~15–18% of revenue. The track record of management meeting their own guidance is mixed — Nokia has a history of downward revisions. That said, the photonic chip expansion is a concrete commitment.

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At $13.49, trailing P/E is an elevated 84× (depressed earnings from restructuring charges), but forward P/E of 28× reflects normalized earnings power. Market cap ~$75B on ~$24B annual revenue.
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $13.49, the market is pricing in roughly **6–8% revenue CAGR with margin expansion to 10–12% operating margin over 5 years**. That is the base case — not demanding, but Nokia has to execute consistently, which it has not always done. At this price, there is a genuine margin of safety if margins recover and optical wins deliver. The forward P/E of 28× is reasonable but not cheap for a company with Nokia's execution history.
- **EV/Sales**: ~3× — reasonable for a hardware/services hybrid. Ericsson trades at similar multiples. Huawei (private) is the competitive ceiling.

## Balance sheet
Nokia carries a moderate amount of cash and has been a net cash position in some recent periods. Pension obligations (Finnish labor law) are a recurring item to watch. Balance sheet is not a stress risk; the Nokia Technologies patent royalty stream provides durable FCF.

## Key metrics to watch
- **Optical/data center networking revenue** (is the AI infrastructure narrative converting to bookings?)
- **Operating margin trend** (watch for 10%+ sustained; anything below 8% is an earnings-recovery miss)
- **Net sales in Mobile Networks** (5G RAN capex cycle — is the bottom in?)
- **Nokia Technologies royalty renewals** (any major contract expiry could compress FCF)
- **Defense (Banshee) contract pipeline** (European defense spending is a real tailwind)
- **Net cash position** (Nokia has been moving toward positive net cash; deterioration is a warning)

- Nokia Technologies loses a major SEP licensing renewal (Apple, Samsung) at materially lower royalty rates
- A major 5G customer (Deutsche Telekom, AT&T, Verizon) publicly selects Ericsson over Nokia for next network cycle
- Optical/photonic data center revenue fails to reach $1B by end-2027 (AI narrative was marketing, not revenue)
- Huawei is allowed back into major Western markets (removes the protected-market tailwind)
