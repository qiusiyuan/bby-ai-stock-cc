# ^DJI — Dow Jones Industrial Average

> **What it is.** The Dow Jones Industrial Average is a price-weighted index of 30 large-cap U.S. blue-chip companies, maintained by S&P Dow Jones Indices. Price-weighting (rather than market-cap) means a high-priced stock like UNH or GS has outsized influence regardless of market cap. No expense ratio — it is an index, not a fund.

## Risks specific to this product
Index risk, not product risk. The price-weighting methodology is the main structural quirk — a single high-priced component (e.g., UNH) can drag or lift the index independently of the broader economy. Only 30 names means idiosyncratic single-stock events show up clearly. Less sensitive to tech momentum than the S&P or Nasdaq.
