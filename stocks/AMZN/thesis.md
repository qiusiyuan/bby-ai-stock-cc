# AMZN — Amazon.com, Inc.

## One-line thesis
Amazon compounds at 15–20% EPS growth through 2028 on AWS cloud re-acceleration + operating leverage in retail, *because* AWS's dominant cloud market position + Bedrock AI infrastructure is benefiting from the same AI capex tailwind as NVIDIA — *this thesis is wrong if* AWS growth decelerates below 15% as Google GCP and Microsoft Azure close the enterprise cloud gap, or if retail operating margins fail to expand as logistics optimization reaches its ceiling.

## Snapshot
Cloud + e-commerce + advertising + AI · Consumer Cyclical / Tech · ~$2.6T cap · founded 1994 · at $244.39 (2026-06-18)

## What it does
Amazon operates three segments: **North America** and **International** (e-commerce, third-party marketplace, advertising, Prime), and **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** (cloud compute, storage, database, AI/ML services). AWS at ~$110B annual revenue run rate is the highest-margin business and the strategic engine — operating margins ~35%, versus retail's 5–7%. Amazon's advertising business ($60B+ annualized) is now the third-largest digital ad platform globally. The company is also expanding into healthcare (One Medical), grocery (Whole Foods + Amazon Fresh), and autonomous delivery (Zoox, drone delivery).

## Industry & TAM
Cloud computing is the foundational infrastructure for the AI era — AWS's TAM grows as AI workloads, data analytics, and enterprise migration continue. The global cloud market is $650B+ by 2026 and growing 18–20%. AWS holds ~31% share. E-commerce is $6T+ globally, with Amazon's North America share around 40%. Advertising is $700B+ globally. The diversification means Amazon has exposure to multiple large TAMs simultaneously, with each feeding the others (Prime keeps shoppers in the ecosystem; their data enriches advertising; the logistics network feeds the marketplace).

## Why I'm watching
Amazon's current valuation (~31× trailing P/E, 25× forward) is reasonable for a company growing earnings at 20%+. The AWS re-acceleration from AI workloads and the advertising business reaching scale are creating operating leverage that the market is only partially crediting. Additionally, Amazon's push to sell Trainium chips externally (June 2026 news) signals ambitions to compete in the AI hardware layer — upside optionality if successful. Prime Day 2026 ($22B GMV expected by BofA) is an upcoming near-term catalyst.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (30%)**: AWS grows 25%+ through 2027 on AI workload demand + Trainium chip sales; retail operating margins reach 8–9% on logistics optimization; advertising hits $80B; advertising + AWS together fund continued expansion. Target $340+.
- **Base (55%)**: AWS grows 18–20%; retail margins expand steadily to 6–7%; advertising grows 15%; Alexa+ adds modest Prime retention value. FCF compounds at 20%+ toward $80–90B by 2028. Target $300.
- **Bear (15%)**: AWS growth decelerates to 12–15% as Azure/GCP close the AI gap; retail margins disappoint on rising wage/logistics costs; macro headwinds hurt consumer discretionary spend. Target $185.

## Moat
Amazon's moat is one of the deepest in the S&P 500, combining multiple independent compounders. **Scale economies** in logistics (Amazon's fulfillment network is effectively a parallel UPS/FedEx) create a structural cost advantage in 1–2 day delivery that cannot be easily replicated. **Switching costs** in AWS are extreme — enterprise cloud migrations take years, and the embedded Bedrock/Sagemaker AI stack creates a new layer of lock-in. **Network effects** in the marketplace (more sellers attract more buyers, more data improves recommendations). The **Prime flywheel** (content + free shipping + pharmacy + grocery + Alexa) is the tightest consumer ecosystem outside of Apple. The weakness: **AWS counter-positioning** — Microsoft's Azure/Office 365 integration and Google's Workspace tie-ins give them structural advantages with their existing enterprise customers that Amazon has to fight for individually.

## Management & capital allocation
Andy Jassy (CEO since 2021, previously AWS CEO) has been disciplined: the 2022–2023 cost-cutting program ($27K layoffs) demonstrated willingness to prioritize profitability. He has executed well on the retail margin expansion story. Jeff Bezos remains Executive Chairman and largest shareholder (~9%), providing continuity of vision. Capital allocation has been aggressive but productive — $75B+ buyback authorization, $75B+ annual capex for AI infrastructure, and disciplined M&A (One Medical $3.9B, iRobot terminated at FTC request). ROIC is expanding strongly, approaching 20%+ on trailing basis.

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At ~$2.6T cap on ~$635B 2025 revenue, that's ~4.1× sales. Trailing P/E 31.5× and forward 24.8× — reasonable given 20%+ EPS growth. AWS alone at a SaaS-style 15× revenue multiple (~$110B ARR) would be worth $1.65T, meaning the retail + advertising businesses trade at ~$1T — essentially free given their combined profitability.
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $244, the market is pricing in roughly **18% FCF CAGR for the next 7 years plus stable margins** — a fair base case with no heroic assumptions. Unlike NVDA, there is genuine margin of safety here if AWS continues its current trajectory.

## Balance sheet
$88B cash, $61B long-term debt — net debt position, but very manageable given $50–60B annual FCF. Credit rating AA. No balance sheet risk.
