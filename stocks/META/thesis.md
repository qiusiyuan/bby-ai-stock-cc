# META — Meta Platforms

## One-line thesis
Meta owns the largest social attention network in human history and is converting it into a durable advertising machine + AI platform, *because* 3.3B+ daily users create a data flywheel no competitor can replicate — *this thesis is wrong if* AI-native assistants erode intent-based discovery (shifting ad spend away from feed/reel surfaces), regulators fragment the Family of Apps, or Reality Labs continues consuming cash without a path to relevance.

## Snapshot
Communication Services · Internet Content & Information · ~$1.47T cap · $577 · 21× trailing P/E · next earnings 2026-07-29

## What it does
Meta runs Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and Threads — collectively reaching ~3.3 billion daily active people. Revenue is almost entirely advertising (97%+ historically), with a small but growing hardware/AI segment via Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses and Quest VR headsets. The company is also pushing Meta AI across all surfaces and building its own AI infrastructure at massive scale.

## Industry & TAM
Global digital advertising was ~$700B in 2025 and growing ~10% annually. Meta has roughly 20–22% of global digital ad revenue, second only to Google. The real question is whether the total pie keeps growing as AI reshapes how users discover content — and whether Meta's surfaces remain the best place for advertisers to reach people. Adjacent TAM in AI-native consumer applications (glasses, wearables, agents) is nascent but potentially large.

## Why I'm watching
Meta is one of the cleanest earnings-compounders in large-cap tech: high free cash flow margins (~40% FCF), disciplined buybacks, and a management team that has repeatedly cut costs when needed (2023 "Year of Efficiency"). The stock is down ~28% from its 52-week high despite improving fundamentals, creating a potential re-entry window.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (30%)**: AI-powered ad ranking continues improving CPMs 10–15%/yr; Meta AI glasses reach 20M+ users by 2028 as a new distribution channel; Reality Labs losses plateau as Quest finds a niche. Revenue compounds at 15–18%/yr. Target $750+.
- **Base (50%)**: Advertising growth moderates to 10–13%/yr as the ad market matures; AI infrastructure spend peaks in 2026–2027 and FCF margins recover; Reality Labs is a slow drag but doesn't break the thesis. Target $650.
- **Bear (20%)**: AI assistants from Apple, Google, and OpenAI disintermediate social feed discovery; ad CPM growth stalls as targeting degrades from further privacy regulation; major antitrust action forces divestiture of Instagram or WhatsApp. Target $400 or below.

## Moat
Meta's moat is primarily **network effects layered with scale economies in data**. Facebook's social graph is 20+ years deep and has survived every "Facebook is dying" cycle — the network value compounds every year more people join. Instagram and WhatsApp reinforce this via different use cases (visual social and messaging, respectively), making cross-app switching costs genuinely high. The **advertising flywheel** is the most durable piece: more users → more behavioral data → better ad targeting → higher CPMs → more revenue to reinvest in product and infrastructure. Rivals cannot easily replicate this data depth. The weak spots: **no cornered resource** in AI model weights (Llama is open-source by design, which is arguably a counter-positioning strategy against OpenAI), and **branding** is permanently complicated by the Facebook name and content moderation controversies. Reality Labs is not a moat — it's an expensive option.

## Management & capital allocation
Mark Zuckerberg holds ~61% voting control via Class B shares, which makes this effectively a founder-controlled company. His track record is genuinely mixed: Instagram and WhatsApp acquisitions were generational value-creates; the metaverse pivot burned $40B+ and destroyed significant shareholder trust. The 2023 Year of Efficiency restored credibility — headcount cut by 21%, operating margins recovered from ~25% to ~40%+. Buyback discipline is strong: Meta has repurchased over $80B in shares since 2022 at disciplined prices. ROIC is high (~30–35% on invested capital). The concentration of voting control is the governance risk, not a day-to-day operational issue.

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At ~$577 and $1.47T market cap, trailing P/E is 21× and forward P/E ~16×. For a business growing revenue 15–20%+ and generating ~40% FCF margins, this is genuinely attractive relative to history (5-year median forward P/E ~22×) and peers. The stock trades ~12% below its 200-day MA — unusual for Meta.
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $577, the market is pricing in roughly **10–12% revenue CAGR for the next decade and operating margins of ~38–40%**. That is a conservative assumption for a business that grew 21% in 2024 and is accelerating AI monetization. The current price implies the market is not fully pricing in the AI tailwind to ad ranking and new surfaces — which is the bull case.

## Balance sheet
Net cash position. Meta holds ~$58B in cash and investments against minimal debt. Free cash flow was ~$52B in 2025 (estimated), giving it full financial flexibility to fund AI infrastructure ($60–65B capex guide for 2026), buy back shares, and weather any regulatory settlement. Balance sheet is not a risk.

- **Meta AI monthly actives** — nascent but the key new surface to watch
