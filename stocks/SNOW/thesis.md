# SNOW — Snowflake Inc.

## One-line thesis
Snowflake is either the AI data layer that every enterprise AI initiative runs through, or an increasingly expensive data warehouse that gets outflanked by Databricks — the thesis is that Snowflake's switching costs and ecosystem are deep enough to capture the AI data platform spend as enterprises move from storing data to running AI on it, but *this thesis is wrong if* Databricks or a hyperscaler-native alternative (BigQuery, Redshift) wins the AI workload migration at the expense of Snowflake's consumption growth, or if consumption growth decelerates to <20% without a credible path to profitability.

## Snapshot
Software - Application (cloud data platform) · NYSE · ~$80B cap · $232 (2026-06-18) · up 66% in 30 days · 52w range $118.30–$284.99

## What it does
Snowflake sells a cloud-based data platform where enterprises store, process, and share data — and increasingly run AI and ML workloads on top of that data. Its core product is a multi-cloud data warehouse (runs on AWS, Azure, GCP) that charges on consumption rather than seats. Key capabilities: Data Cloud (cross-cloud data sharing + Marketplace), Cortex AI (native LLM and ML functions inside Snowflake), Snowpark (Python/Java/Scala development inside the warehouse), and Data Clean Rooms (privacy-preserving data collaboration). The OpenAI partnership (announced 2025) enables joint enterprise customers to embed AI directly into Snowflake workflows.

## Industry & TAM
The cloud data platform market is roughly $50B today and expanding as AI workloads require managed, governed data at scale. Snowflake's secular tailwind is the "data gravity" thesis — AI models run best close to the data, and Snowflake holds massive enterprise data estates. The Databricks rivalry is real: Databricks raised at a $62B valuation in 2024 and has been aggressively winning machine learning and data engineering workloads. The hyperscalers (BigQuery, Redshift, Synapse) are a ceiling on pricing power and a constant competitive threat, but Snowflake's multi-cloud portability and data-sharing marketplace provide differentiation they cannot easily replicate. Jefferies (June 2026) sees room for both Snowflake and Databricks to expand simultaneously from growing enterprise demand.

## Why I'm watching
The 66% move in 30 days requires an explanation. Snowflake has been repositioning from "just a data warehouse" to "AI Data Cloud" — Cortex AI, the OpenAI partnership, and investments like Jedify (autonomous context graphs for agentic AI, funded June 2026) signal that Snowflake is trying to be the data + AI layer, not just the storage layer. If enterprises decide to run AI agents directly on Snowflake's platform (query data, trigger models, output results — all inside one governed environment), the consumption growth could re-accelerate meaningfully. The 30-day move suggests the market is beginning to price this possibility.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (25%)**: Cortex AI + OpenAI partnership drives agent/AI workloads to Snowflake; consumption growth re-accelerates to 35%+ in FY2027; NRR expands back above 130%; path to GAAP profitability becomes visible. Target $340+.
- **Base (50%)**: Snowflake maintains 20–25% consumption growth; Cortex AI adds meaningful but not transformative revenue; Databricks competition contained; adjusted FCF margin improves. Target $260–280.
- **Bear (25%)**: Databricks wins AI/ML workloads at accelerating pace; hyperscaler native tools commoditize warehouse compute; consumption growth drops below 20%; CEO/management turnover creates uncertainty; GAAP profitability path unclear. Target $140 or below.

## Moat
Snowflake's moat is **switching costs layered with ecosystem lock-in**. Enterprise data estates are deeply embedded in Snowflake schemas, stored procedures, and Snowflake SQL conventions — migrating terabytes of structured data and rewriting pipelines takes months and carries material risk. The **Data Marketplace** (3,000+ data providers and 500+ data apps) creates a network effect that is genuinely sticky: once your data ecosystem is built around sharing with partners via Snowflake, leaving requires your partners to leave too. **Counter-positioning**: Snowflake's multi-cloud architecture is genuinely difficult for any single hyperscaler to replicate, because each hyperscaler has incentive to make their own offering the default. This creates a durable independent niche. The **weak spot** is Databricks, which has built a compelling open-source Lakehouse architecture (Delta Lake, MLflow) that avoids Snowflake's proprietary lock-in — this is a real threat for data-science-led organizations who value openness over Snowflake's governance model.

## Management & capital allocation
Sridhar Ramaswamy has been CEO since February 2024 (replacing Frank Slootman). Ramaswamy comes from Google (led Google Ads, then Neeva). He has the AI product credibility Snowflake needs but is early in his tenure and has not yet proven the AI piviot will drive consumption re-acceleration. Capital allocation is growth-focused: heavy sales/marketing and R&D spending, no dividend, share-based compensation is a persistent dilution headwind (~10–12% of revenue). The shift from hyper-growth (2020–2022) to more deliberate AI-platform positioning is rational but uncertain.

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At $232, trailing P/E is not meaningful (GAAP net loss). Forward P/E of 87× on adjusted estimates. ~$80B cap on ~$3.6–3.8B estimated FY2027 revenue (fiscal year ends January). Price/Sales ~21× — expensive.
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $232, the market is pricing in roughly **25–30% revenue CAGR for the next 5 years plus eventual FCF margins of 25–30%**. That is achievable if Cortex AI drives consumption growth re-acceleration. Without AI workloads re-accelerating the consumption model, the base case is that the stock is fairly valued at best and priced for bull outcomes at worst. The 66% 30-day run has pulled forward significant future value; buying here requires conviction on the AI pivot.

## Balance sheet
Snowflake carries net cash and has substantial remaining performance obligations (RPO, a proxy for committed future revenue). FCF has been positive on an adjusted basis. The balance sheet is not a stress. However, GAAP profitability is not expected near-term, and SBC dilution is material — track share count growth carefully.

## Key metrics to watch
- **Product revenue growth YoY** (consumption-based; acceleration vs. deceleration is the primary signal)
- **Net revenue retention rate** (NRR; was ~128% in early 2024, needs to stabilize or expand)
- **Remaining performance obligations (RPO) growth** (forward committed revenue; a leading indicator)
- **Cortex AI consumption as % of product revenue** (how much of growth is from AI workloads?)
- **Snowflake Marketplace data product listings** (ecosystem health)
- **GAAP operating margin trajectory** (closing the gap to profitability; watch SBC as % of revenue)
