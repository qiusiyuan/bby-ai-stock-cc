# SQQQ — ProShares UltraPro Short QQQ

> **What it is.** SQQQ is a **3x daily inverse** ETF targeting -300% of the Nasdaq-100's daily return. ProShares rebalances the fund's swap/derivative positions every single trading day to reset the 3x leverage — this means the fund's long-term returns are not simply -3x the Nasdaq's return over any multi-day period. In trending or volatile markets, daily rebalancing causes **compounding decay (beta decay / volatility drag)**: the fund loses value over time even if the Nasdaq ends flat. SQQQ is a **short-term trading instrument only**. Expense ratio: 0.95%. AUM: ~$2.5B.

## Risks specific to this product
**Decay risk is the defining risk.** ProShares' own prospectus states these funds "are not designed for investors who hold them for periods longer than a single day." In a rising or sideways Nasdaq, SQQQ loses value every day. The 52w high of $115.80 vs. current $36.75 illustrates the magnitude: while QQQ has risen through 2026, SQQQ has declined ~68% from its 52w high. Holding this for macro hedging over weeks or months is a losing strategy in bull markets. Expense ratio of 0.95% adds to the drag. P/C ratio of 0.22 confirms this is overwhelmingly used by traders who are long the fund (not writing puts against it).
