# TSLA — Tesla, Inc.

## One-line thesis
Tesla's bull case is entirely dependent on Full Self-Driving autonomy and Robotaxi monetization becoming commercial reality by 2027–2028, *because* the core EV business at 367× trailing P/E is already priced for transformative optionality — *this thesis is wrong if* FSD fails to achieve regulatory approval in major markets, Robotaxi launches repeatedly slip, or Elon Musk's continued involvement in politics causes irreversible brand damage in Tesla's core markets.

## Snapshot
Electric vehicles + energy + AI/autonomy · Consumer Cyclical · ~$1.5T cap · founded 2003 · at $400.49 (2026-06-18)

## What it does
Tesla designs, manufactures, and sells battery electric vehicles (Model 3, Y, S, X, Cybertruck) and energy storage systems (Powerwall, Megapack). It also operates a global Supercharger network, provides FSD (Full Self-Driving) software as a subscription, and is developing a Robotaxi/Cybercab platform. Energy generation and storage is a growing second business with Megapack for grid-scale installations. The company is vertically integrated in battery, manufacturing, and software in a way legacy automakers cannot easily replicate.

## Industry & TAM
The EV market is transitioning from early-adopter to mass market, with EV penetration at ~20% of new car sales globally and expected to reach 40–50% by 2030. The real optionality is in autonomy: if Tesla's FSD achieves Level 4/5 approval, the Robotaxi TAM (ride-hailing, fleet services) is $1–2T annually. Megapack addresses the utility-scale energy storage market, growing at 30%+ annually as renewable energy buildout accelerates.

## Why I'm watching
No other public company combines an operating EV business with the optionality of a potentially transformative autonomy platform at scale. The stock is either massively overvalued relative to a car business, or fairly valued if Robotaxi/FSD are credited. The binary nature of the autonomy bet makes this a conviction position to size carefully and monitor actively.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (25%)**: FSD v13/Unsupervised gets regulatory approval in 3+ U.S. states and several EU countries by end-2027; Cybercab/Robotaxi generates $5B+ revenue in 2028; Megapack scales to $25B/yr. Target $700+.
- **Base (40%)**: FSD remains supervised-mode; Robotaxi enters limited commercial deployment in 1–2 cities by 2028; EV volumes recover to 2M+ units/yr; Megapack continues 30–40% growth. Target $450.
- **Bear (35%)**: FSD fails EU approval (Sweden veto a live risk), Robotaxi delayed beyond 2028; EV competition from Chinese OEMs (BYD, Xiaomi) + Tesla brand erosion from Musk political controversy causes volume declines; margins compress further. Target $200.

## Moat
Tesla's moat is real but narrower than bears or bulls think. **Switching costs** for existing Tesla owners are genuine — ecosystem lock-in (Supercharger network, OTA software, FSD subscription investment). **Scale** in manufacturing is real but BYD now outsells Tesla on volume. The **brand** is bifurcating: strong with technology-first buyers globally, under pressure from Musk political associations particularly in Europe. The most defensible long-term moat is the **data flywheel** from 6M+ vehicles collecting real-world driving data — this is NVIDIA-level process power applied to autonomy. If that data lead translates to FSD superiority, it's a cornered resource. If Waymo or the Chinese AV players close the gap, the moat narrative breaks.

## Management & capital allocation
Musk is one of history's great product/manufacturing visionaries, but his governance posture is among the most concentrated and unpredictable in the S&P 500. He holds ~13% equity but effectively controls decision-making. The $56B pay package was controversial and initially rejected by Delaware courts. His concurrent leadership of SpaceX, X, xAI, and Department of Government Efficiency creates real time and attention risk. Tesla's ROIC has declined from 30%+ to low single digits as capex intensity rose and margins compressed. Capital allocation since 2022 has been less disciplined — the Cybertruck launch was expensive and volumes remain modest. Robotaxi capex commitment is the next major test.

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At ~$1.5T cap, trailing P/E is 367× and forward P/E is 160×. This is not a car company valuation — it is a full pricing of the Robotaxi/autonomy option. Compared to purely auto peers (Toyota, BYD, GM), the premium is 20–40×.
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $400, the market is pricing in roughly **30% CAGR for 7–8 years plus margin expansion from ~5% today back to 20%+**, which requires Robotaxi to be substantial. Without autonomous monetization, the stock is worth $150–200 on auto-only fundamentals. You are paying $250/share for the option value of autonomy.

## Balance sheet
$37B cash, minimal debt, free cash flow recently recovered. Capex is high but well-funded. No financial stress. The only balance sheet watch item is that free cash flow is sensitive to whether FSD/Robotaxi capex is expensed vs. capitalized and timing of those investments.

## Key metrics to watch
- **FSD regulatory approvals** — EU (Sweden blocking is the live canary), California, Texas
- **Vehicle deliveries** (currently ~1.8M/yr; BYD comparison is the competitive gauge)
- **Automotive gross margin ex-credits** (was ~17% in Q1 2026; floor matters)
- **Megapack GWh deployed** (energy storage growth rate)
- **Cybercab prototype fleet size and Robotaxi launch milestones**
- **Musk political controversy headlines** — EU brand sentiment surveys are an early warning

## Catalysts (next 12 months)
- Q2 2026 earnings (July 22, 2026) — first print after Robotaxi event
- Cybercab commercial launch announcement (expected late 2026/early 2027)
- EU FSD regulatory decision — TCMV ruling on Sweden's objection
- Optimus robot production ramp (first commercial deployments expected 2026)
- China market volumes — MIIT quarterly data on EV market share

## Disconfirming events (would make me sell)
- FSD/Robotaxi timeline slips to beyond 2028 without a credible new milestone
- EU-wide FSD rejection triggers formal regulatory restriction
- Musk-driven governance event: board replacement, another pay controversy, or conflict with Tesla shareholders
- Automotive gross margin ex-credits falls below 15% for two consecutive quarters
- BYD or a Chinese OEM establishes a meaningful foothold in North America EV market
