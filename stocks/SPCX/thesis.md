# SPCX — SpaceX

## One-line thesis
In 18–24 months, SPCX will compound at >25% rev growth on Starlink subscriber economics + Starship cargo-ramp, *because* the launch monopoly + 75% of active maneuverable satellites creates compounding scale advantages no competitor can replicate near-term — *this thesis is wrong if* Starlink ARPU collapses below $50, Starship fails to reach commercial cargo cadence by 2027, or Musk's voting control triggers a governance event that breaks fiduciary trust.

## Snapshot
Vertically integrated space platform · Aerospace/Comm Services · ~$2.4T cap · IPO'd 2026-06-12 · max weight TBD

## What it does
Three business lines under one roof:
1. **Launch services** — Falcon 9/Heavy (operational, 661/664 success rate) and Starship (in development, world's largest reusable rocket). Launches commercial satellites, NASA crew/cargo, DoD/NRO national-security payloads.
2. **Starlink** — ~10,400 satellites, ~12M subscribers across 160 countries, providing satellite broadband. Now SpaceX's largest segment.
3. **AI** — added in Feb 2026 via xAI acquisition (Grok, X, GPU clusters); plans for space-based AI compute.

## Industry & TAM
Three overlapping markets. **Global commercial launch** is ~$10–15B/yr, growing 8–12% — SpaceX already had ~45% share back in 2017. **Satellite broadband** (the real growth engine) — Starlink's $11B 2025 revenue serves ~12M of an addressable >1B underserved-broadband households globally; aviation, maritime, and direct-to-cell expansions push TAM into telecom-adjacent territory ($50B+/yr by 2030 most reasonable estimate). **AI compute** is the wild card — added late, hard to size, partially defensive against Tesla's positioning.

## Why I'm watching
First mega-cap pure-play on space + satellite broadband economics, with a unique vertical integration story (own launch, own constellation, own user terminals). It's the only public way to express the thesis that LEO-broadband becomes a utility-scale business this decade.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (25%)**: Starship reaches reliable cargo cadence by 2027; Starlink hits 30M subs by 2028 with stable $60+ ARPU; aviation/maritime/D2C add high-margin verticals. Target $300+.
- **Base (50%)**: Starlink 20M subs by 2027 at $55 ARPU; Starship still semi-experimental but progressing; xAI integration messy but value-additive. Modest re-rate. Target $220.
- **Bear (25%)**: Starship suffers another 18-month delay; Starlink ARPU compresses below $50 from emerging-market mix; Kuiper/OneWeb chip into share; Musk concentration triggers governance discount. Target $130 or below.

## Moat
The strongest moat is **process power layered with scale economies**. Reusability cost-per-kg is roughly an order of magnitude below Western competitors and 3–5× below Chinese state launchers. **Switching costs** are real on the constellation side — Starlink has 75% of active maneuverable satellites in orbit, an asset that takes a competitor 5–10 years and $20B+ to replicate (Kuiper is 4 years behind on satellites in orbit). **Cornered resource**: USML/ITAR clearance + Musk's regulatory access act as a barrier. The weak spots: no real **counter-positioning** (incumbents like ULA can copy reusability eventually), and **branding** is largely Musk-personal — which can flip from asset to liability.

## Management & capital allocation
Musk holds 42% equity, 85% voting control via super-voting stock — Harvard governance scholars have flagged this as unusually entrenched. Track record on capital allocation is mixed: SpaceX itself is a stunning operational success, but the xAI acquisition (Feb 2026, all-stock at $1.25T combined) was widely critiqued as related-party value transfer from SpaceX shareholders to Musk's other holdings. Insider ownership is enormous; insider selling discipline post-IPO is the single biggest governance variable to watch. Buyback discipline: untested (just IPO'd).

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At ~$2.4T cap on $18.7B 2025 revenue, that's ~128× sales — extreme. P/E negative (still loss-making at the consolidated level, though Starlink alone earned $4.4B operating income in 2025). Forward P/E of -2055 reflects continued Starship-driven losses.
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $185, the market is pricing in roughly **35–40% revenue CAGR for the next decade plus operating margin expansion to 25%+**. That is achievable only if (a) Starlink hits 50M+ subs at sustained $60+ ARPU and (b) Starship reaches commercial cargo. Both are individually plausible; jointly, that's the bull case being priced as if it were the base case. **Valuation is the most aggressive part of this thesis** — buying here is paying for outcomes that aren't yet proven, with no margin of safety.

## Balance sheet
$92.1B total assets, ~$86B raised in IPO. Net loss of $4.9B in 2025 means ongoing burn from Starship; Starlink's $4.4B operating income is the cash engine that funds it. Debt levels low post-IPO. Leverage isn't the risk; it's whether the cash engine outpaces the development burn, which it currently does.

## Key metrics to watch
- **Starlink subscribers** (currently 12M; was 8.9M six months ago — slope matters more than the level)
- **Starlink ARPU** (currently $66, down from $99 in 2023 — declining due to emerging-market mix; floor matters)
- **Starlink operating margin** (currently 39% — does it hold or expand?)
- **Starship flight cadence + payload-to-orbit success rate**
- **Insider net selling post-IPO lock-up expiry**
- **Aviation/D2C revenue mix** (high-margin, currently ramping with United/Lufthansa/IAG deals)

## Catalysts (next 12 months)
- First post-IPO earnings print (likely Q3 2026 or Q4 2026)
- Starship V3 ongoing flight test cadence (May 2026 was 12th flight, Raptor 3 first)
- Direct-to-cell rollout milestones (T-Mobile commercial, India approval pending)
- Vietnam licensing follow-through (Feb 2026 license, ~600K terminals planned)
- Lock-up expiry — typically 90/180 days post-IPO

## Disconfirming events (would make me sell)
- Starship suffers a major catastrophic failure that delays cargo capability by >18 months
- Starlink ARPU drops below $50 across two consecutive quarters
- Net insider selling exceeds $5B in any 90-day window post-lockup
- Major regulatory action breaks the Starlink-in-conflict-zones playbook (e.g., FCC revokes spectrum, multi-country bans)
- Musk-driven related-party transaction reduces SpaceX shareholder economics in a material way
- Kuiper or another competitor demonstrates reusable-launch parity with material customer wins
