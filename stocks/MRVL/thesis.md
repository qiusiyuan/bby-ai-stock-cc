# MRVL — Marvell Technology, Inc.

## One-line thesis
Marvell is the lead design partner for AWS Trainium (Amazon's custom AI chip) and a dominant supplier of optical interconnect for AI clusters — the thesis is that custom silicon plus high-speed optical networking is a faster-growing and more durable franchise than GPU alternatives, and at $310 MRVL is still early in a multi-year re-rate — *this thesis is wrong if* AWS moves Trainium design in-house, optical interconnect becomes commoditized, or the TSMC 1.4nm partnership delays threaten Marvell's product roadmap.

## Snapshot
Semiconductors · NASDAQ · ~$272B cap · $310.58 (2026-06-18) · +7.3% today · +80% in 30 days · 52w range $61.44–$329.88 · upcoming S&P 500 inclusion

## What it does
Marvell designs data infrastructure semiconductors. Its three primary businesses are: **Custom Silicon (AI ASIC / XPU)** — design and supply of custom AI training/inference chips for hyperscalers, most prominently AWS Trainium; **Optical Interconnect** — high-speed SerDes, DSPs, silicon photonics, co-packaged optics, and linear pluggable optics for data center interconnects (this is the fastest-growing part); and **Traditional Data Infrastructure** — storage controllers (HDD/SSD), ethernet NICs, PCIe switches, Fibre Channel adapters. The company is fabless (TSMC and Samsung manufacture). The Teralynx T100 switch (102.4 Tbps, unveiled June 2026) is the latest product targeting AI/cloud data center fabrics.

## Industry & TAM
Marvell operates at the intersection of two structural growth markets. **Custom AI silicon (XPU)** for hyperscalers is a market that Broadcom and Marvell jointly dominate — hyperscalers want cost and power efficiency specific to their model architectures, which merchant GPUs cannot deliver. AWS Trainium is Marvell's largest customer relationship, and the news that Amazon plans to sell Trainium chips externally (June 2026 catalyst) is a step-function increase in the Trainium TAM and therefore Marvell's potential revenue per chip cycle. **Optical interconnect** for AI data centers is the highest-growth product line — AI clusters with 100K+ GPUs require extraordinary bandwidth between compute nodes, and silicon photonics is the solution being adopted at scale. The $10B+ AI networking market (Nvidia now claiming #1 spot per June 2026 news) is the arena Marvell competes in through its interconnect products.

## Why I'm watching — key recent developments
Three converging catalysts explain today's 7.3% move and the 80% 30-day run:
1. **Amazon plans to sell Trainium externally** (June 2026): Marvell is the lead design/manufacturing partner. External Trainium sales would be incremental revenue on top of Amazon's own internal use — a direct expansion of the total silicon volume Marvell processes.
2. **TSMC 1.4nm partnership** (announced around June 2026): Marvell committed to using TSMC's most advanced process node for next-generation products. This locks in leading-edge manufacturing access and signals both confidence in the product roadmap and TSMC's willingness to prioritize Marvell.
3. **S&P 500 index inclusion**: Passive fund buying is a mechanical near-term demand driver. MRVL was at $61 52-weeks ago — this inclusion represents legitimization of the re-rate.
4. **Teralynx T100 switch**: Industry's first 102.4 Tbps AI/cloud data center switch, positioning Marvell in the high-end AI fabric networking segment.

## Outlook — Bull / Base / Bear
- **Bull (30%)**: External Trainium sales scale meaningfully by 2027; optical interconnect wins accelerate across all major hyperscalers; TSMC 1.4nm products ship on schedule; additional custom AI chip customer wins (beyond AWS). Target $420+.
- **Base (50%)**: AWS Trainium remains the core anchor customer; optical interconnect grows 50%+ CAGR through FY2027; S&P 500 inclusion provides index-demand support. Target $330–350.
- **Bear (20%)**: Amazon in-houses Trainium design incrementally, reducing Marvell's design revenue per chip; optical faces commoditization pressure; TSMC 1.4nm faces delay; AI data center capex cools. Target $180 or below.

## Moat
Marvell's moat is narrower than Broadcom's but real and growing. **Switching costs** are the primary source: hyperscalers co-develop custom silicon with Marvell over 2–3 year design cycles; switching costs are enormous in time and IP continuity. The AWS relationship is a particularly sticky moat because Trainium is deeply integrated into AWS's MLOps infrastructure (SageMaker, EC2 P/Trn instances). **Process power**: Marvell's optical interconnect IP is deep — coherent DSPs, silicon photonics integration, co-packaged optics — these are genuinely complex to replicate and represent years of engineering investment. The **weak spot**: Marvell is more customer-concentrated than Broadcom (AWS is a very large revenue source), and unlike Broadcom, there is no VMware-equivalent software cash engine providing diversification. New entrants like Architect Labs ($24M seed, June 2026 — explicitly targeting Marvell's custom chip business) are worth watching over a 3–5 year horizon.

## Management & capital allocation
Matt Murphy (CEO since 2016) has executed a clean transformation of Marvell from a diversified hardware company (storage, networking, mixed quality) to a focused data infrastructure play. The Inphi acquisition (optical interconnect, 2021, $10B) has been the defining deal — it gave Marvell the optical IP that is now the fastest-growing segment. Capital allocation has been disciplined: divested consumer business segments, focused R&D on high-value data center problems. ROIC was low historically due to the transitional nature of the business but is on a steep upward trajectory as AI-related revenue scales.

## Valuation
- **Multiples**: At $310, trailing P/E of 107× (depressed earnings base). Forward P/E of 50× on estimates reflecting rapid earnings recovery. ~$272B cap on ~$7–8B estimated FY2026 revenue (fiscal year ends Jan).
- **Reverse-DCF**: At $310, the market is pricing in roughly **40–50% revenue CAGR for the next 3 years with operating margin expanding from current ~20% to 35%+**. The 80% 30-day move has pulled forward significant future value. This is achievable if AWS Trainium external sales materialize and optical interconnect keeps scaling — but it requires execution. At current price, there is meaningful execution risk embedded in the valuation. The KeyBanc 48% price target hike (June 2026) and the stock's recent run may reflect the bull case being partially priced in.
- **Important caveat**: MRVL's fiscal year ends in January, so "FY2026" corresponds to the year ended January 2026. Revenue and earnings estimates need to be mapped carefully to calendar-year comparisons.
