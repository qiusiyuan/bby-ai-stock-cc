# SK Hynix (SKHY) — Investment Thesis

## Company

SK Hynix is one of three global memory manufacturers alongside Samsung and Micron. Headquartered in Icheon, South Korea with ~48,000 employees, the company produces DRAM (server, mobile, PC, graphics), NAND flash, and SSDs. It's the undisputed leader in HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) for AI accelerators — the sole qualified supplier for NVIDIA's H100/H200/B200 HBM3E and the frontrunner for HBM4.

**IPO context:** SKHY listed on Nasdaq on 2026-07-10, raising $26.5B at $149/share — the second-largest US IPO ever (behind SpaceX). Opened at $170 (~+14%). This is a direct ADR listing from the Korea Exchange (000660.KS), giving US investors direct access to the HBM cycle's clearest winner.

## Industry & TAM

The global DRAM market is ~$100B+ annually (2025-2026), with HBM growing from ~$15B in 2024 to an expected $50B+ by 2027. SK Hynix commands ~50%+ of HBM production share and ~25-30% of total DRAM. CEO Kwak Noh-jung stated publicly on IPO day that 2027 will be "the worst year in the industry's history from the supply perspective" — demand will outstrip capacity well into 2030+. This is the tightest memory supply setup in a decade.

## Moat

SK Hynix's defensibility rests on two pillars:

1. **Cornered resource / process power:** HBM manufacturing requires proprietary TSV (through-silicon via) stacking + advanced packaging. SK Hynix's yield advantage on HBM3E (reportedly 80%+ vs Samsung's 50-60%) is the result of 5+ years of process refinement that can't be replicated quickly. This isn't just a capital question — it's institutional know-how embedded in the production line.

2. **Switching costs / customer lock-in:** NVIDIA validated SK Hynix as sole HBM3E supplier and co-developed HBM4 specifications. Changing memory vendors for a given GPU generation requires 12-18 months of qualification. Once designed in, you're locked for that product cycle.

Samsung is the only credible challenger, but the 30d performance spread of 40+ percentage points in mid-2026 tells you the market sees years of catch-up ahead.

## Management & Capital Allocation

CEO Kwak Noh-jung (since March 2024) has been aggressive on HBM capacity expansion — $28B+ capex planned over 2025-2027. The IPO itself is a capital allocation statement: raising $26.5B for capacity build-out when the equity window is at its widest. SK Group (parent chaebol) provides balance sheet backing but also introduces Korean governance complexity (cross-shareholdings, chaebol dynamics).

## Valuation

**Forward P/E:** ~4.9x (based on KRX listing consensus, ~₩2.18M / ₩445K forward EPS equivalent). This is extraordinarily cheap for a global monopoly-adjacent position — cheaper than Micron's ~15x, reflecting Korean discount + IPO-day pricing dynamics.

**Reverse-DCF read:** At $170 (ADR opening price), the market is pricing in roughly 25-30% earnings growth sustained for 3 years followed by a normalization — well below most HBM demand forecasts that project 40-50% CAGR through 2027. The ADR appears to be pricing the base case conservatively given the supply shortage thesis.

**Korean discount caveat:** The KRX-listed shares have historically traded at 4-6x forward vs global peers at 10-15x. The US listing could partially close this gap as passive flows and institutional mandates (many US funds can't buy Korean domestic shares) drive re-rating.

## Balance Sheet

Net debt position is manageable given the massive capex cycle. The $26.5B IPO proceeds provide substantial runway. No near-term solvency concern — this is a company that went from bailout candidate (2012) to $1.5T market cap.

## Key Metrics to Watch

1. **HBM production share** — must stay >50% (currently dominant)
2. **HBM3E/HBM4 yield rates** — the moat lives here
3. **Samsung qualification progress** — any NVIDIA validation of Samsung HBM3E is a code-red
4. **Conventional DRAM ASP trajectory** — monthly DRAMeXchange spot pricing
5. **US ADR premium/discount to KRX** — tracks liquidity arbitrage convergence
6. **Capex execution** — $28B commitment must translate to actual capacity online
