# DRAM — Roundhill Memory ETF

> **What it is.** DRAM is an actively managed ETF from Roundhill Investments providing concentrated exposure to global memory chip and storage companies — the companies that make DRAM, NAND flash, and related memory technologies. The fund holds both equity positions and total-return swaps to access non-US-listed names (e.g., SK Hynix, Samsung). Expense ratio: 0.65%. AUM: ~$17.5B (one of the fastest-growing ETFs in 2026). ## Why I'm watching
Direct expression of the AI memory trade. HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) demand from AI accelerator buildouts is the single biggest tailwind for DRAM and NAND pricing. DRAM's basket covers the three major memory players (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) plus adjacent names (Kioxia, SanDisk, Seagate). When AI capex rips — as it is now — memory prices lead. Today's +9.66% was driven by Apple flagging "unavoidable" memory price hikes, confirming margin pressure is being passed through the supply chain.

## Risks specific to this product
**Concentration risk is extreme**: effectively three companies (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) are ~67% of the fund. A single pricing cycle downturn — memory oversupply returning — would crater the fund. The swap structure introduces counterparty risk and tracking complexity. No 200-day MA yet (fund launched April 2026) — price history is very short; the +57% 30-day run is exceptional but lacks the context of a full cycle. Korean/Japanese foreign exposure means FX and geopolitical risk (Korea-DPRK, US-China tech export controls). Roundhill is a smaller issuer; fund closure risk is non-zero if AUM retreats.
